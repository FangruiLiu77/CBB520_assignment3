Features

Gene Description

SIX6 (SIX Homeobox 6) gene is one of the homeobox transcription factors in the SIX/sine oculis family. It is located on Chromosome 14 (14q23.1), linked to SIX1 and SIX4. The corresponding protein is a homeobox protein similar to the product of the Drosophila ‘sine oculis’ gene in fly’s visual system. The protein has a divergent DNA-binding homeodomain and an upstream SIX domain, which are assumed to play a role in DNA-binding and protein-protein interactions.

Expression and Features

SIX6 has a high level of expression in human in the cilary body, choroid, sclera, optic nerve head, and retina. It is believed to be associated with early eye development. Specifically, it is suggested that SIX6 regulates early retinal progenitor cell proliferation as part of the eye development.

Relationship with Glaucoma

SIX6 locus has been associated with the development of primary open-angle glaucoma (POAG), which is a leading cause of blindness worldwide. It is believed that SIX6 gene variants lead to increased POAG susceptibility by suppressing retinal ganglion cell development, which subsequently results in reduced number of retinal ganglion cells in adulthood. The reduction in the number of retinal ganglion cells during adulthood hastens the development of glaucomatous optic neuropathy as it naturally decreases with aging. One common nonsynonymous SNP of SIX6 known as rs33912345 is found to alter SIX6 protein, leading to an increase in vertical cup-disc ratio (VCDR), which can lead to POAG.
